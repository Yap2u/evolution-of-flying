Grammar Focus
  • 1. Present Perfect: “Flying cars have captured our imagination…” — Use present perfect to talk about actions that started in the past and continue now.
  • 2. Passive Voice: “Challenges need to be addressed.” — Use passive voice to focus on the action, not the doer.
  • 3. Modals of Possibility: “...may soon become a normal part of everyday life.” — Use “may,” “might,” and “could” to show future possibility.
  • 4. Comparative Adjectives: “...quieter, more efficient…” — Use comparatives to show differences between options.
  • 5. Future with “Will”: “...flying cars will be common in cities.” — Use “will” for predictions.
  • 6. Relative Clauses: “...vehicles that can take off vertically.” — Use “who,” “that,” “which” to add detail about a noun.
  • 7. Infinitives: “...attempting to combine automobiles and aircraft.” — Use “to + verb” to express purpose or action.
  • 8. Past Simple: “Designs dated back to the 1910s.” — Use simple past to describe completed actions in the past.
